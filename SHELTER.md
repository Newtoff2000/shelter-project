# Shelter Research — Ericeira Paws

> Supplementary to [SSOT.md](./SSOT.md). This document captures research about the shelter, its community, and the six key user journeys the website must support. Update as we learn more from the shelter team and users.

## 1. About Ericeira Paws

Ericeira Paws is **not a private rescue organisation**. It is a group of international volunteers operating *inside* the Mafra municipal public shelter (CROAMM — Centro de Recolha Oficial de Animais do Município de Mafra). This distinction matters: the animals live in government-run cages under austere public-sector conditions. The volunteers are not running a cosy rescue house — they are showing up to a hard environment and making it less hard.

The organisation was founded by a group of people from different countries who ended up in Ericeira and decided to act on what they saw. The team is international by nature, English-speaking, and community-driven.

### Key facts

| Fact | Detail |
|---|---|
| Animals in shelter | ~45 dogs, ~10 cats |
| Volunteer days | Monday, Wednesday, Friday, 9am–1pm |
| Outdoor time per animal | 15–20 minutes per session — the **only** time they leave their cages |
| Shelter distance from Ericeira | ~15-minute drive |
| Instagram | [@ericeira.paws](https://www.instagram.com/ericeira.paws/) (~1,650 followers) |
| GoFundMe | €2,042 raised of €3,000 goal from 33 donors (as of research date) |
| 3Horas partnership | Listed on [3horas.org/paws](https://3horas.org/paws/) — a platform connecting expats to local causes |

### Conditions the animals face

- Fed low-quality food with poor nutritional value
- Sleep on wet, dirty wood palettes
- No vitamin supplements (poor coat and dental health visible)
- Limited medical treatment — public budget is minimal
- New arrivals come in regularly, sometimes in very poor condition
- No outdoor time unless volunteers show up

### What the organisation provides

- Weekly walks and outdoor time for each animal
- Supplementary food, vitamins, and bedding funded by donations
- Vet visits coordinated by volunteers for fostered animals
- Adoption and foster matching
- Social media presence that drives adoption enquiries

## 2. The Ericeira Context

Ericeira is a small coastal town (~10,000 population), 40 minutes north of Lisbon. It is:

- **Europe's only World Surf Reserve** — seven world-class waves within 4 km
- A major **digital nomad and expat hub** — a wave of international residents, remote workers, and surfers in their 20s–40s
- Strongly international — English is widely spoken, and foreign residents integrate actively into local community life
- Connected to Lisbon's expat scene but with a slower, community-centred rhythm

**This matters for the website.** A significant portion of potential adopters, volunteers, and donors are international residents or visitors. Many are transient (here for months, not years), making **fostering** as important an outcome as adoption. Many first encounter Ericeira Paws not through a local Portuguese-language search, but through Instagram or word of mouth in the expat community.

### Adoption in Portugal — what users need to know

Portugal's municipal shelters are often underfunded and overcrowded. Adopting through Ericeira Paws means:

- Small donation (typically €40–150)
- Animal must be microchipped and registered in SIAC
- Vaccinations required (rabies included)
- Pet passport available for EU travel (€20–50)
- Rental restrictions can be a real obstacle for expats — landlord's written permission needed
- Animals often arrive with trauma; adjustment takes time and patience (one adopter's dog "had never seen or heard a car")

Fostering costs less upfront — the foster is responsible for food, basic necessities, and medical care during the fostering period. Ericeira Paws supports fosters throughout.

| Mode | Commitment | Who it suits |
|---|---|---|
| **Adopt** | Lifetime | Settled residents, families, people with stable housing |
| **Foster** | Weeks to months | Digital nomads, expats in flux, people not ready to adopt permanently |
| **Walk dogs** | 3–4 hrs, Mon/Wed/Fri | Local residents, Ericeira community members, short-term volunteers |
| **Donate** | One-off or recurring | Anyone, anywhere — especially people who can't be present |
| **Social media / content** | Flexible | People with video/photo skills |
| **Event coordination / fundraising** | Project-based | Organisers, community connectors |
| **Core team** | Regular, ongoing | People committed to the mission long-term |

## 4. Six Key User Journeys

These journeys are ordered by volume and emotional urgency. They inform page architecture, copywriting, CTAs, and the priority of features.

---

### Journey 1 — The Adopter

**Who:** A local Portuguese family, a settled expat, or someone deeply moved by a specific animal they saw online.

**Motivation:** Wants a companion; wants to rescue rather than buy. May have been thinking about it for a while, or may be acting on impulse after a reel.

**Entry point:** Instagram → website; direct Google search; recommendation from friend or volunteer.

**Goal:** Find the right animal and initiate the adoption process.

**Step-by-step:**
1. Lands on the homepage; hero creates an immediate emotional hook and establishes trust
2. Scrolls to the animal feed; uses filters (species, size, age group, gender)
3. Opens an individual animal profile — reads personality, history, sees photos and video
4. Hits "I'm Interested" → pre-filled contact form sends to shelter inbox
5. Receives reply from team; arranges visit; meets animal in person
6. Adoption is formalised through the municipal shelter (CROAMM)

**What this journey demands from the design:**
- The **animal profile page is the single most important page on the site** — rich media, warm personality-first copy, nothing bureaucratic
- Filters must be fast, intuitive, and work on mobile
- "I'm Interested" CTA must be prominent and low-friction (one tap on mobile)
- A brief "What happens next?" note after the form reduces abandonment anxiety
- Full bilingual support: many adopters read in English first
- Adopted animals should show a success state, not simply disappear

---

### Journey 2 — The Foster

**Who:** A digital nomad or expat temporarily living in Portugal; a local who wants to help but can't commit to permanent adoption; someone responding to a specific animal's fostering need.

**Motivation:** Wants to make a meaningful impact; understands they can't adopt permanently (leaving in 3 months, renting with restrictions, etc.); fostering feels achievable.

**Entry point:** Instagram reel featuring a foster story; 3Horas listing; recommendation from the volunteer WhatsApp group.

**Goal:** Understand what fostering means; find an animal to foster; contact the team.

**Step-by-step:**
1. Hears about fostering from Instagram or community network
2. Lands on site; actively looks for "fostering" content
3. Reads a clear, honest explanation of what fostering involves (time, costs, support provided)
4. Browses animals available to foster (ideally a dedicated filter or flag)
5. Contacts the team; joins the volunteer WhatsApp group for guidance
6. Fosters the animal; later (ideally) shares their own story

**What this journey demands from the design:**
- Fostering must have **its own visible section**, not buried under general adoption
- Copy must reduce the fear of commitment: "temporary," "we support you," "even a few weeks helps"
- Foster success stories (e.g., Odin's foster story) are the most powerful conversion tool — feature them prominently
- Practical detail: what costs are involved, what support is available, what happens at the end
- If the user is not ready to foster, gently redirect to volunteering or donating — don't lose them

---

### Journey 3 — The Volunteer

**Who:** An expat or local looking to integrate into the community and give their time; often discovers Ericeira Paws through 3Horas, Instagram, or a friend already volunteering.

**Motivation:** Wants purpose, community, and contact with animals; has time but may not have permanent housing for a pet; wants to give something back to the place they're living.

**Entry point:** 3Horas platform → Ericeira Paws listing; Instagram; a friend in the WhatsApp group.

**Goal:** Understand how to get involved; join the team.

**Step-by-step:**
1. Discovers the cause through 3Horas, Instagram, or word of mouth
2. Lands on site; looks for "how to help" or "volunteer"
3. Sees the different roles clearly: dog walker, social media, events, core team
4. Understands the practical logistics: Mon/Wed/Fri, 9am–1pm, shelter is 15 min from Ericeira, 3–4 hrs per session
5. Contacts the team via form or joins the WhatsApp group to get started

**What this journey demands from the design:**
- **Multiple volunteer paths** with different time commitments and skill requirements — not everyone can walk dogs
- Practical info front and centre: schedule, location, what to expect on your first day
- Real photos of volunteers with animals (not stock) — the community feel must be authentic
- Low-barrier first step: joining the WhatsApp group requires no commitment
- Address the "I'm only here for 2 months" hesitation — make it clear that short-term help still matters
- A volunteer's first visit should be easy to arrange; don't hide the logistics

---

### Journey 4 — The Donor

**Who:** A remote supporter who can't be physically present — a past Ericeira visitor, a social media follower in another country, a diaspora member, or a global animal-lover who found the cause online.

**Motivation:** Emotionally moved by the cause; wants to help but geography or circumstances prevent active volunteering. Wants to know their money does something real.

**Entry point:** Instagram post → link in bio → homepage; GoFundMe link shared in a post or story.

**Goal:** Donate confidently; feel their contribution has a concrete impact.

**Step-by-step:**
1. Sees a post or reel showing the animals' conditions or a success story
2. Clicks through to the site; quickly needs to understand the cause and its legitimacy
3. Sees a clear breakdown of what donations pay for (food, vitamins, beds, vet visits)
4. Clicks the Donate / GoFundMe CTA → completes donation
5. Ideally: shares the page with their network or follows for future updates

**What this journey demands from the design:**
- **Donate CTA must be in the top bar and in the hero** — visible without scrolling
- Short, punchy "what your donation does" breakdown: bullet points, not paragraphs
- Social proof: donation count, amount raised, specific impact ("your €20 buys a month of vitamins for one dog")
- Transparency is core to Ericeira Paws' identity — lean into it explicitly
- Mobile-optimised: this journey likely starts and ends on a phone
- Don't bury GoFundMe deep in the page — donors have short attention windows

---

### Journey 5 — The Instagram Impulse Visitor

**Who:** Someone who saw a specific dog or cat in a reel or carousel on Instagram and tapped the link in bio. They have a strong emotional reaction to one animal and want to find them right now.

**Motivation:** Pure emotional impulse — a specific face, a specific story, a specific video. The window for action is narrow.

**Entry point:** Instagram reel or post → link in bio → homepage.

**Goal:** Find that specific animal on the site; get more information; act (adopt, share, donate) before the impulse fades.

**Step-by-step:**
1. Sees a reel of a specific animal on Instagram (e.g., name in the caption or on-screen)
2. Taps link in bio → lands on homepage
3. Scans the animal feed looking for that specific animal by name or appearance
4. Opens the animal profile; watches the video; reads the story
5. Acts immediately: "I'm Interested" form, shares the profile, or donates

**What this journey demands from the design:**
- Animal feed must **load fast and be instantly scannable** — cover photos, names, key trait chips
- Name-based text filter is critical at this scale — don't make them scroll through 50 animals
- Individual animal pages need proper **OG meta tags** for rich link previews when shared on WhatsApp, iMessage, or social media
- After the "I'm Interested" CTA, surface Donate and Share options — capture the emotional peak while it's there
- **Mobile-first throughout** — this journey is 100% phone-based
- If the animal is already adopted, show it gracefully with a success story — never a dead end

---

### Journey 6 — The Community Champion

**Who:** A past volunteer, a previous adopter, a regular donor, or a dedicated follower who has an existing relationship with the shelter and its animals.

**Motivation:** Wants to stay connected to the community; proud of the impact they've been part of; wants to share progress with friends and encourage others to get involved.

**Entry point:** Bookmarked site; link shared in the volunteer WhatsApp group; Instagram story; notification about a new success story.

**Goal:** See success stories; feel the impact of their involvement; share updates with their network.

**Step-by-step:**
1. Returns to site out of habit or prompted by a new post
2. Looks for success stories — adopted animals, foster reunions, dogs who've been at the shelter for years and finally found a home
3. Feels emotional connection and pride
4. Shares a success story with friends or social network
5. May re-engage: donate again, volunteer again, encourage a friend to visit

**What this journey demands from the design:**
- **Success Stories section is load-bearing for this journey** — not optional or a nice-to-have
- Adopted animals shown with brief stories, names, and photos ("Odin found his forever home in March")
- Rich social sharing: every animal profile and success story card should be shareable with a good preview
- Sense of an ongoing, living community — animals change, stories unfold, impact accumulates
- Progress signals: number adopted this month, total adoptions, current animals waiting — makes the mission feel real and active
- Returning users should immediately see what's new; a "recent arrivals" or "just adopted" signal helps

## 5. Design Principles (Derived from Journeys)

| Principle | Why |
|---|---|
| **Mobile-first** | Instagram is the primary discovery channel; most users arrive on a phone |
| **Emotional before informational** | The cause is emotional by nature — lead with faces, names, and stories, not lists and facts |
| **Speed and scannability** | Impulse visitors have a narrow window; feed and filters must be instant |
| **Radical transparency** | Ericeira Paws' core promise — show where money goes, show real conditions, show real outcomes |
| **Bilingual without friction** | Seamless PT-PT / EN switching — no broken layouts, no missing strings |
| **Multiple on-ramps** | Not everyone can adopt; every page should offer a graceful next step (adopt → foster → volunteer → donate → share) |
| **Real over polished** | Real photos of real animals and real volunteers; avoid anything that looks like a stock-photo charity campaign |

## 6. Open Research Questions

These should be answered before finalising design:

- [ ] What does the formal adoption process look like end-to-end through CROAMM? (For the "What happens next?" post-form explainer)
- [ ] Does the team want a WhatsApp link as a primary CTA for volunteering, or do they prefer the contact form?
- [ ] What is the actual GoFundMe embed/link strategy — link out, or embed the widget on the page?
- [ ] Can we get real volunteer + adoption photos for the design? (Critical — we cannot design this with stock)
- [ ] Are there specific animals who have been waiting a long time and should be featured prominently?
- [ ] Success Stories: will the shelter team be able to update these in Sanity, or should they be auto-populated from `status = adopted`?
